# Types of Networks, Internet, Intranet, and Extranet

---

## 1. Types of Networks

### Definition

A type of network refers to the classification of a computer network based on its geographical coverage, the number of devices it connects, the speed of data transmission, and the ownership of the network.

### Key Points

- Networks are classified based on the area they cover and the devices they connect.
- Different types of networks are designed to serve different purposes and environments.
- The four main types of networks are:
  1. PAN (Personal Area Network)
  2. LAN (Local Area Network)
  3. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
  4. WAN (Wide Area Network)

---

### 1.1 PAN (Personal Area Network)

#### Definition

A Personal Area Network is the smallest type of network that connects devices within the immediate vicinity of a single person, typically within a range of 10 meters, for personal use.

#### Key Points

- PAN is centered around an individual person and their personal devices.
- It connects devices such as smartphones, laptops, tablets, smartwatches, wireless earphones, and keyboards.
- PANs can be wired (USB cable) or wireless (Bluetooth, Infrared, Zigbee).
- The most common technology used in wireless PAN is Bluetooth.
- Range is very limited — typically up to 10 meters.
- Data transfer speed is relatively low compared to other network types.
- PANs are generally set up by the user themselves without any professional network configuration.

#### Advantages

1. **Easy to Set Up** - No complex configuration required; devices connect automatically via Bluetooth or USB.
2. **Low Cost** - No additional infrastructure or cabling is needed.
3. **Portable** - Moves with the person since it covers only their personal space.
4. **Low Power Consumption** - Bluetooth and similar technologies consume very little power.

#### Disadvantages

1. **Very Limited Range** - Works only within a few meters; not suitable for larger areas.
2. **Low Data Transfer Speed** - Not suitable for transferring large files quickly.
3. **Limited Number of Devices** - Can connect only a small number of devices at a time.
4. **Interference** - Bluetooth signals can be affected by other wireless devices and physical obstacles.

#### Examples

1. A smartphone connected to wireless Bluetooth earphones.
2. A laptop connected to a wireless Bluetooth mouse and keyboard.
3. A smartwatch syncing health data with a smartphone via Bluetooth.
4. A laptop connected to a mobile phone via USB for internet tethering.

---

### 1.2 LAN (Local Area Network)

#### Definition

A Local Area Network is a type of network that connects computers and devices within a limited geographical area such as a home, office, school, or building, typically covering a range of up to 1 kilometer.

#### Key Points

- LAN is the most commonly used type of network in homes, offices, schools, and colleges.
- It covers a small and well-defined area such as a single building or a campus.
- LANs are privately owned and managed by the organization or individual.
- Data transfer speed in a LAN is very high — typically 100 Mbps to 10 Gbps.
- Two common technologies used in LAN:
  1. **Ethernet (Wired LAN)** - Devices connected using cables (twisted pair, fiber optic).
  2. **Wi-Fi (Wireless LAN / WLAN)** - Devices connected wirelessly using radio waves.
- Common topologies used in LAN: Star, Bus, Ring.
- A switch is the primary device used to connect computers in a LAN.

#### Advantages

1. **High Speed** - LANs provide very high data transfer speeds compared to other network types.
2. **Low Cost** - Setting up and maintaining a LAN within a building is relatively inexpensive.
3. **High Security** - Since LAN is privately owned, access can be tightly controlled.
4. **Resource Sharing** - Printers, files, and internet connections can be shared easily among all devices.
5. **Low Error Rate** - Short distances and good quality cables result in very low data errors.

#### Disadvantages

1. **Limited Coverage Area** - Covers only a small geographical area.
2. **Limited Number of Devices** - A single LAN has a practical limit on the number of devices it can support efficiently.
3. **Maintenance Required** - Requires regular maintenance of cables, switches, and configurations.
4. **Security Risk if Misconfigured** - If not properly secured, unauthorized users within the area can connect.

#### Examples

1. A computer network in a college lab connecting 30 computers to a central switch and a shared printer.
2. A home network connecting a laptop, desktop, smart TV, and mobile phones to a Wi-Fi router.
3. An office network connecting all employee computers to a central file server.

---

### 1.3 MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

#### Definition

A Metropolitan Area Network is a type of network that covers a larger geographical area than a LAN, typically spanning an entire city or a group of nearby buildings within a range of 10 to 100 kilometers.

#### Key Points

- MAN connects multiple LANs spread across a city or town into a single larger network.
- It is larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN.
- MANs are typically owned and operated by government bodies, ISPs (Internet Service Providers), or large organizations.
- Common technologies used in MAN: Fiber optic cables, WiMAX (Worldwide Interoperability for Microwave Access).
- Data transfer speed in MAN is moderate to high — typically 10 Mbps to 1 Gbps.
- MANs are more expensive to set up than LANs due to the larger infrastructure required.

#### Advantages

1. **Covers a Large Area** - Connects devices and networks spread across an entire city.
2. **High Speed** - Provides faster connectivity than WAN for city-wide communication.
3. **Shared Infrastructure** - Multiple organizations can share the same MAN infrastructure, reducing individual costs.
4. **Supports Large Number of Users** - Can serve thousands of users across the city.

#### Disadvantages

1. **High Setup Cost** - Requires significant investment in infrastructure (cables, towers, equipment).
2. **Complex Management** - Managing a city-wide network is more complex than a LAN.
3. **Dependent on Service Provider** - Organizations often depend on telecom companies for MAN services.
4. **Security Challenges** - Larger coverage area means more potential points of vulnerability.

#### Examples

1. A city-wide network connecting all branches of a bank within the same city.
2. A cable TV network providing internet and TV services to multiple neighborhoods in a city.
3. A network connecting all government offices within a state capital city.
4. University campus network connecting multiple buildings and departments spread across a large campus.

---

### 1.4 WAN (Wide Area Network)

#### Definition

A Wide Area Network is a type of network that covers a very large geographical area, such as a country, continent, or the entire world, by connecting multiple LANs and MANs together over long distances.

#### Key Points

- WAN is the largest type of network in terms of geographical coverage.
- The internet is the largest and most well-known example of a WAN.
- WANs span countries, continents, and even the entire globe.
- They are typically owned and operated by telecommunication companies, ISPs, or government bodies.
- Common technologies used in WAN: leased lines, satellite links, fiber optic undersea cables, MPLS (Multiprotocol Label Switching).
- Data transfer speed is generally lower than LAN and MAN due to the large distances involved.
- Routers are the primary devices used to connect networks in a WAN.
- WANs are more expensive to set up and maintain than LAN and MAN.

#### Advantages

1. **Very Large Coverage** - Connects devices and networks across countries and continents.
2. **Enables Global Communication** - Allows people and organizations worldwide to communicate and share data.
3. **Centralized Data Management** - Organizations with branches in multiple countries can manage data from a central location.
4. **Supports Internet** - The global internet is built on WAN infrastructure.

#### Disadvantages

1. **High Cost** - Very expensive to set up and maintain due to the large infrastructure required.
2. **Lower Speed** - Data transfer speeds are lower compared to LAN due to long distances.
3. **High Latency** - Data takes longer to travel across long distances, resulting in higher delay (latency).
4. **Complex Security** - Securing a network spread across multiple countries is very challenging.
5. **Dependent on Telecom Providers** - Organizations rely on ISPs and telecom companies for WAN connectivity.

#### Examples

1. The Internet — connecting billions of devices across the entire world.
2. A multinational company's private network connecting its offices in India, USA, UK, and Japan.
3. ATM networks of banks that allow customers to withdraw money from any ATM across the country.
4. An airline's reservation system connecting all booking offices and airports around the world.

## 2. Internet, Intranet, and Extranet

---

### 2.1 Internet

#### Definition

The Internet is a global, publicly accessible network of networks that connects millions of computers, servers, and devices worldwide using the TCP/IP protocol suite, enabling communication, data sharing, and access to information on a global scale.

#### Key Points

- The internet is the largest WAN in the world.
- It is a public network — accessible to anyone with a device and an internet connection.
- No single organization or government owns or controls the entire internet.
- It connects billions of devices across every country in the world.
- The internet is built on a standard set of protocols — primarily TCP/IP.
- Services available on the internet include: World Wide Web (WWW), email, file transfer, video streaming, online gaming, VoIP, and cloud computing.
- Users connect to the internet through ISPs (Internet Service Providers) such as Jio, Airtel, BSNL.

#### Uses of the Internet

1. **Communication** - Email, instant messaging, video calls (Gmail, WhatsApp, Zoom).
2. **Information Access** - Browsing websites, reading news, searching on Google.
3. **E-Commerce** - Online shopping on Amazon, Flipkart.
4. **Entertainment** - Streaming on Netflix, YouTube, Spotify.
5. **Education** - Online courses, digital libraries, virtual classrooms.
6. **Banking** - Net banking, UPI payments, online transactions.
7. **Cloud Computing** - Accessing data and applications stored on remote servers.

#### Example

A student in Rajkot can access MIT's online courses, watch YouTube lectures, send emails to professors, and submit assignments — all through the internet, without any geographical limitation.

---

### 2.2 Intranet

#### Definition

An Intranet is a private network that is owned and used exclusively by an organization, accessible only to authorized employees or members of that organization, and uses the same internet technologies (TCP/IP, HTTP) but is not accessible to the general public.

#### Key Points

- An intranet is a private, internal network — not accessible from outside the organization without authorization.
- It uses the same technologies as the internet (web browsers, HTTP, TCP/IP) but is restricted to internal users only.
- Access to an intranet is controlled through user authentication (username and password).
- Employees can access the intranet from within the office network or remotely through a VPN.
- An intranet may or may not be connected to the internet.
- Common uses within an organization: internal websites, employee portals, document sharing, internal announcements, HR systems.

#### Uses of Intranet

1. **Employee Information Portal** - HR policies, company announcements, salary slips, and leave applications.
2. **Internal Communication** - Internal email, messaging, and discussion forums for employees.
3. **Document and File Sharing** - Employees share project files, reports, and presentations through the intranet.
4. **Training and Learning** - Internal training materials, videos, and e-learning modules for employees.
5. **Project Management** - Tracking project progress, task assignments, and deadlines internally.

#### Example

A company like Infosys has an intranet portal where employees can log in to check their salary slips, apply for leave, access internal company policies, and collaborate on projects — all within the company's private network. An outsider cannot access this portal from the internet.

---

### 2.3 Extranet

#### Definition

An Extranet is a controlled private network that allows authorized external users such as business partners, suppliers, vendors, or customers to access specific parts of an organization's intranet over the internet in a secure manner.

#### Key Points

- An extranet is an extension of an intranet that is selectively opened to specific external parties.
- It is not accessible to the general public — only to authorized external users with proper credentials.
- External users access the extranet through the internet, typically through a secure VPN or HTTPS connection.
- The organization controls which parts of its intranet are visible to external users.
- External users can only see the data and resources that have been specifically shared with them — not the entire intranet.
- Common technologies used: VPN, SSL/TLS, firewalls, user authentication.

#### Uses of Extranet

1. **Supplier and Vendor Portal** - Suppliers check inventory levels and submit invoices through the extranet.
2. **Customer Portal** - Customers track their orders, view invoices, and access support services.
3. **Partner Collaboration** - Business partners collaborate on joint projects by sharing documents and data through the extranet.
4. **Distributor Access** - Distributors check product availability and place orders through the company's extranet.

#### Example

A manufacturing company shares its production schedule and inventory data with its raw material suppliers through an extranet portal. The suppliers log in securely to check how much material to deliver and when — without having access to any other internal company data.

### 2.4 Difference Between Internet, Intranet, and Extranet

| Feature | Internet | Intranet | Extranet |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accessibility | Public — accessible by anyone worldwide | Private — accessible only to organization's employees | Semi-private — accessible to authorized external users only |
| Ownership | No single owner | Owned by the organization | Owned by the organization |
| Users | General public | Employees of the organization | Selected external partners, vendors, customers |
| Security | Lower (public network) | Higher (private, internal) | Moderate (secured access for external users) |
| Purpose | Global communication and information access | Internal communication and resource sharing | Collaboration with external partners and stakeholders |
| Access Control | No restriction (open to all) | Username and password for employees | Username and password for authorized external users |
| Connection | Available globally through ISPs | Within organization's network | Through internet with secured authentication |
| Example | Google, YouTube, Amazon | Company HR portal for employees | Supplier portal of a manufacturing company |

## Important Definitions for Exam (Quick Revision)

- **PAN (Personal Area Network):** The smallest type of network that connects personal devices of an individual within a range of up to 10 meters, typically using Bluetooth or USB.

- **LAN (Local Area Network):** A network that connects computers and devices within a limited area such as a building or campus, covering up to 1 km, with high speed and private ownership.

- **MAN (Metropolitan Area Network):** A network that covers a city or group of nearby buildings within a range of 10 to 100 km, connecting multiple LANs using fiber optic or WiMAX technology.

- **WAN (Wide Area Network):** A network that spans a very large geographical area such as a country or the entire world by connecting multiple LANs and MANs using leased lines, satellite, or fiber optic links.

- **Internet:** A global, publicly accessible network of networks that connects billions of devices worldwide using TCP/IP protocols, enabling communication, information sharing, and access to online services.

- **Intranet:** A private network owned by an organization that uses internet technologies but is accessible only to authorized employees within that organization.

- **Extranet:** A controlled private network that extends an organization's intranet to allow authorized external users such as suppliers, vendors, or customers to access specific resources securely over the internet.

- **ISP (Internet Service Provider):** A company that provides internet access to individuals and organizations. Example: Jio, Airtel, BSNL.

- **WiMAX:** Worldwide Interoperability for Microwave Access; a wireless communication technology used to provide high-speed internet over large areas, commonly used in MAN.

- **Latency:** The time delay experienced during data transmission from source to destination across a network; higher in WAN due to long distances.

- **VPN (Virtual Private Network):** A secure, encrypted connection over the internet that allows remote users to access a private network (intranet or extranet) as if they were physically connected to it.

- **Leased Line:** A dedicated, permanent telephone or data connection between two locations, used in WAN to ensure a fixed bandwidth private connection for an organization.

- **Bluetooth:** A short-range wireless technology used in PAN to connect personal devices such as headphones, keyboards, and smartphones within a range of approximately 10 meters.
